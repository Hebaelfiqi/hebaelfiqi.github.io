Courses at UNSW Canberra

Undergraduate

ZEIT4150 Fundamentals of Artificial Intelligence

A tour of the breadth of artificial intelligence: search, knowledge representation, reasoning, planning, and natural language processing. Students learn to formulate real-life problems as well-defined AI problems, design and validate AI solutions, and communicate AI challenges, solutions, and ethics to non-technical audiences.

The course is hands-on throughout, from problem-solving with state-space search to designing and implementing an adversarial game-playing agent and an individual AI project. By the end, students hold the foundations of the field, ready for advanced study or a fourth-year thesis in AI.

Undergraduate

ZEIT4151 Machine Learning

Hands-on machine learning for engineering, computer science, and cyber security students: mapping real-life problems to mathematical models machines can optimise and learn from, interpreting how data shapes what a model learns, and judging which algorithm suits which problem and why.

Students build and validate working systems through applied projects that progress from model validation and feature extraction to machine learning with images, delivered as code with explanation so the reasoning matters as much as the result.

Postgraduate · online

ZEIT8601 Applied Machine Learning

Built for postgraduates across cyber security, decision analytics, space, project management, and systems engineering: when and how to apply machine learning to real problems. Students master learning from data, feature selection, regression, classification, and clustering, and the differences between supervised and unsupervised learning, including bias in machine learning.

The course works across modalities, from tabular data to text and images, and closes the loop that matters in practice: evaluating models statistically and communicating findings in a form decision makers can act on.

Leadership

Building AI education, not just delivering it

As AI Discipline Coordinator (2021 to 2023) I oversaw the design, resourcing, and first deliveries of the new AI course stream in the engineering program, and I later joined the faculty AI Education Strategy Workgroup that shaped the new postgraduate AI courses introduced in 2024, leading the development of the first of them, Applied Machine Learning. I have also served on the assessment panels for fourth-year Artificial Intelligence engineering projects.

Approach

Accessible by design

I design layered, multi-level learning resources so students at different starting points can all engage with advanced material, structuring each course to build from foundations toward authentic, applied problem-solving. This approach has earned consistently strong student satisfaction.
